Music
Sun Bleached Flies by Ethel Cain

A manifesto for radical agency, finding the grace in active participation and the quiet power of accepting life's shifting terrain.

Time to Pretend by MGMT

The necessity of a "perfect pop song", where easy listening meets a communal feeling of angst and aspiration to create a shared, universal resonance.

Hard Feelings / Loveless by Lorde

A study of the beautifully difficult, using the symbolism of daily rituals to navigate raw emotion, self-love, and the courage required for self-recovery.

Imagery
Two Citizens by Salman Toor

An intimate study of the intersection of queerness and race, exploring how the complexities of identity and cultural adherence define a "place of belonging" far beyond the borders of official citizenship.

Window / Carvaggio by Wolfgang Tillmans

A shift from Tillman's iconic youth culture to the quiet exploration of light, shadow, and mortality following the passing of his partner due to AIDS — capturing the profound stillness in grief and the heartbreaking fragility of the life we share.

Books

An immersive story in world-building and the tragic pursuit of happiness, revealing that our only true safety lies in the miracle of human connection and the radical necessity of helping one another.

Just Kids by Patti Smith

A raw exploration of love, loss, and the artistic dream that prioritizes the act of living and witnessing over the hollow pursuit of accolades or wealth.

"Everyone had something to offer, and nobody appeared to have much money."

A New Earth by Eckhart Tolle

A study in objective observation, teaching the value of detaching from our own ideas / ego to achieve a deeper, more unified understanding of the human experience.

"There is nothing either good or bad, but thinking makes it so." (Shakespeare, Hamlet)
